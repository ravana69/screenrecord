How to screenshot a Chrome page

Let’s start with Movavi ScreenShot – a handy screen capture Chrome extension.

Movavi ScreenShot advantages:

  • Takes a screenshot in Google Chrome on Windows 10 and Mac.
  • Makes a screen grab at multiple sizes: full page screenshots, specific parts of the screen, or full screen, including all open tabs.
  • Requires no special permissions for installation.
  • Can be used in incognito mode.
  • Supports free downloading screenshots of Chrome.

Extension disadvantages:

  • No editing tools
  • No link to share screenshots via social networks

Here’s a short guide of how to print-screen Chrome with Movavi ScreenShot.

Step 1. Install the extension

To install the extension, find the Movavi ScreenShot extension in Google Web Store and hit the Add to Chrome button.

Step 2. Choose the size

Check that the ScreenShot icon has appeared in the top right corner of your browser.

Hit the screenshot icon and choose the size of the capture area:

  • Full page screenshot. Capture the entire webpage without scrolling. Movavi ScreenShot will scroll the page automatically even if it's a mile long.
  • Full screen. Grab the visible screen area, including control panel, open windows, tabs, and messengers.
  • Selected parts. Frame the area you want to capture. This way, you won’t have to edit the image later to cut out unwanted parts.

When you’ve chosen the size, hit the appropriate icon and capture the website page.

Step 3. Take a screenshot on your PC

Wait for a few seconds until the capturing process is complete. Save the screenshot to your computer, choosing an appropriate folder.

The image will be saved in JPG format by default. Now you can edit the Google Chrome screenshot whenever you wish or upload it to the cloud.

How to screenshot in Google Chrome with Developer Tools: Method 1

Next way is taking a screenshot with developer tools. Actually, there’re two ways. We’ll describe both, and you can choose the most appropriate one.

But first, let’s check the pros and cons of using the developer tools.

    Pros:
  • Requires no installation.
  • Lets you customize the size of your screenshot.
  • Has a specific screenshotting option for developers.
    Cons:
  • Takes time to find the right setting.
  • Many-step process.
  • May be difficult for inexperienced users.
  • No editing tools.
  • No sharing option.

Now we'll give you a guide on how to screenshot in the Chrome browser.

Step 1. Open the Developer Tools tab

Click on the three dots in the right top corner of the browser and go to More Options. Choose the last item – Developer Tools.

You can also use shortcuts: Alt + Command + I on Mac or Ctrl + Shift + I on Windows.

Step 2. Go to the Device Toolbar

Find the icon with two devices on it and hit it.

Step 3. Choose the size

Now you should define the size of the screenshot. You can set any image parameters or just choose one of the standard options in the Responsive menu.

Step 4. Capture the screenshot

Click on the three dots by the size settings. Choose one of the options – Capture full size screenshot or Capture screenshot – depending on your needs.

If you choose Capture full size screenshot, the entire page will be screenshotted. The second option – Capture screenshot – is for taking a simple photo of the current screen.

Choose the folder and save the image to your desktop. You can edit it later.

Taking a Chrome screenshot with Developer Tools: Method 2

Let us describe the second method to take a Google Chrome screenshot with developer tools. It's one of the easiest ways to take a screenshot on a PC in Google Chrome.

Step 1. Open Developer Tools

Go to Developer Tools again.

Now press the shortcuts: Ctrl + Shift + P for Windows or Command + Shift + P on Mac. The search area will appear.

Step 2. Choose the screenshotting option

Type “screenshot” in the search field. The browser will offer you four options with different image sizes. Choose the appropriate one.

You may get confused by the Capture node screenshot option. What is this for? It grabs a screenshot of an HTML element selected in the Element Inspector. Usually, this function is mainly used by developers, designers, and QA engineers.

Step 3. Take a screenshot

Press the selected option and let Chrome take the screenshot. Save it to your computer.

How to take a screenshot in Google Chrome on an iPhone

It may be not obvious how to take a Google Chrome screenshot on an iPhone. Usually, people capture the entire screen and then delete the phone frames in the editing app. If you’re one of those people, you’ll be surprised to learn that there’s another way of taking a screenshot in the Google browser.

Step 1. Activate the AssistiveTouch setting

Open the General iPhone settings and find the Accessibility section. Here you’ll see AssistiveTouch. Activate it.

Step 2. Run the screenshot tool

Open the Google page you want to capture. Click on the AssistiveTouch icon that’s in the bottom right corner of the screen. Find the Device button and press it.

Click on the three dots to open the More menu.

Step 3. Take the screenshot in Chrome

Press Screenshot and wait for a second.

The image will appear on the left side of the screen. Swipe it to the left. It will be automatically saved to your photos. Now you can add text, filters, and animations in any editing app.

How to print screen Chrome on Android

There are a couple of ways to print screen a Chrome page on Android.

Go to the settings on your phone and look for a Smart Capture function. It should be in the advanced settings menu.

If there’s no Smart Capture tool, look for the screen recording icon in the quick settings. To open the quick settings, swipe down twice from the top of your screen. There should be a circle icon. If you have it, you’re in luck!

If not, you just can press the Power and Volume down buttons and take a screenshot of the entire screen. After that, you can edit the image by cutting out the unnecessary parts.
