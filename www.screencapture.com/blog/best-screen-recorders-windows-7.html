Online platforms to record a screen on Windows 7

Screencapture.com

Start Recording

Best for: no-download screen recording and sharing

Rating: 4.7/5 stars, 406 good reviews

Screencapture.com, also known as Screen Capture, is a free online Windows 7 video recorder. You can load it up in your favorite browser, like Chrome, Opera, Edge, or Firefox, and use it to record your various windows and screen activities. It's completely free to use, without any downloads required and has strong security and privacy policies to protect your files. It saves all videos in HD, and you can also use mic and webcam recording.

Features:

  • Record the entire screen or a specific part
  • Grab footage from webcam simultaneously
  • Record system sound, microphone, or both

Pros:

  • Completely free to use
  • No downloads or installations
  • Very simple
  • Works on all major browsers

Cons:

  • Watermark on your recordings when using the free version

Conclusion

We hope this article has been helpful. In any case, here is a list of things to consider when choosing a recording tool:

Features: If you simply want to record your screen, any recorder will most likely suffice. If you need some editing tools or streaming functions, take those features into account.

Simplicity: Choose software that fits your level of experience. Some programs are simple but don't do much. Others have more features but have a steeper learning curve.

OS: Make sure that the recorder you choose supports the OS of your device.

Support: Try to find a program that offers easily accessible user support if you run into problems.

Reviews: See what people have to say about the app you are going to use. Users may point out issues and help you avoid them.

Frequently asked questions

How to record a screen on Windows 7?

  1. Visit Screencapture.com to get started.
  2. Adjust the settings to suit your preferences, and click Start Recording to begin.
  3. Your browser will ask for permission to access your webcam and mic – allow this permission to continue.
  4. Press Stop when you've finished recording to save your file.

Does Windows 7 have a screen recorder?

Windows 7 does not have any kind of default or built-in screen recorder. However, there are many ways to record your screen for free on Windows 7 devices, such as using the Screen Capture online tool or the Movavi Screen Recorder desktop app.
