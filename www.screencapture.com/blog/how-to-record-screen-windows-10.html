Desktop software to record screen on Windows 10

One of the best ways to record a screen on Windows 10 is to use a desktop program. It provides high quality output and offers a wide range of recording features. You can record screen and audio simultaneously, which is convenient for making video tutorials and presentations.

On the other hand, the majority of desktop programs have a long launch process compared with online tools. Here’s a list of the best recorders that capture video with sound. Check them out and learn how to record a video on Windows 10.

Movavi Screen Recorder is a simple yet powerful screen recording program. It captures your screen, system sounds from your laptop, voice from your microphone, and webcam images.

The app has a wide range of settings that will expand your options for creating educational videos. For example, you can highlight cursor clicks or display your keyboard. It’s useful for recording video tutorials on using specific programs.

You can also schedule your recordings. This is a favorite feature of anyone who frequently records webinars and streaming videos.

2. OBS Studio

Best for: creating professional video clips

OS: Windows 8.1, 10, 11; macOS 10.13 or later

Price: Free

TechRadar rating: 4 out of 5 stars

OBS is free software that is highly regarded by professional video creators. It allows users to set up an unlimited number of scenarios and custom transitions to seamlessly link them. When it comes to audio, users can also benefit from the audio mixer that includes a number of per-source filters including noise suppression, noise gate, and gain.

If you ask about disadvantages of the program, the first thing that comes to mind is its challenging interface. Indeed, OBS has so many settings that you risk going crazy searching for the one you want. We would not recommend starting with this program if you don’t need to make a professional-grade video clip.

Another issue is a lack of technical support.

3. Xbox Game Bar

Best for: computer gameplay recording

OS: Windows 10, 11

Price: Free

To screen capture video on Windows 10, you can use the Xbox Game Bar. It’s a built-in Windows tool that is designed for recording game streaming videos. But you can also use it as a simple screen recorder.

Note that using Game Bar, you can capture only a single app. It’s not possible to record the whole desktop or file explorer. You cannot customize the capture area. The length of recording is also limited.

The program does not include any editing tools. It's basic screen and audio recorder on Windows 10.

Advantages of Game Bar:

  • Requires no installation
  • Simple to use
  • Perfect for game recording on Windows

Disadvantages of the built-in app:

  • Recording time is limited.
  • No opportunity to customize the capture area size.
  • No advanced recording tools.

Online video captures on Windows 10

Now let’s talk about online video grabbers. They became popular in recent years because they don’t require installation on your computer.

It’s quick to launch an online recorder. But the danger of capturing screens online is that many recording services scan your browser data, which could be seen as a security risk. Of course, this does not apply to all online recorders.

Here’s our top list of online screen recorders. Choose any one of these and you can be sure nobody will steal your data!

1. Screen Capture

Best for: secure recording

OS: any (web-based)

Price: Free version; full version costs $9.95 per month

Trustpilot rating: 4.5 out of 5 stars, 75 reviews

Start Recording

Screen Capture has a simple interface and clear settings. It’s suitable even for inexperienced users because it’s obvious how to use it.

You can record any tab on your screen, including windows on your desktop. If you want to record your voice on a webcam, you can do this separately from the screen recording. This function is often used by bloggers and performers.

Screen Capture can be used in Incognito mode if you have privacy concerns. But the service guarantees that all your recordings are secure. Nobody can see your videos until you share them yourself.

The only thing you may not like about Screen Capture is the watermark on videos in the free version. To remove the watermark, you can buy a subscription which is very reasonably priced.

Advantages of Screen Capture:

  • Secure recording
  • Quick start to the recording process
  • Webcam can be recorded separately from screen recording

Disadvantages of the online tool:

  • Small watermark in the free version

Browser extensions to make a video screen capture on Windows 10

Browser extensions are very convenient when you need to make a quick screen capture and share it as fast as possible. That’s why browser extensions are so popular among remote workers. You don’t need to write emails with long explanations – just record your screen and demonstrate what you want to say.

But the significant disadvantage of all extensions is that they have more crashes and bugs than desktop and online programs. This is related to constant browser updates and changes in operating systems requirements.

1. Screencastify

Best for: online drawing on videos

Supported browsers: Google Chrome

Price: Free

Google Web Store rating: 4 out of 5 stars, 11,500+ reviews

Users: 10,000,000+

Screencastify captures your desktop, browser tab, and webcam video. You can also add voice narratives and comments on the video. In the preview window, there are simple editing tools such as trimming, merging, and cropping recordings.

But the really unique feature is an online drawing tool. You can activate the pen tool during the recording process!

Screencastify provides convenient sharing and exporting options such as uploading to cloud storage, publishing to YouTube, sharing videos via Google classroom, or saving to the desktop in MP4, GIF, or MP3.

But many users have complained that they cannot log into their account and download recordings. Another issue is that Screencastify requests private Google Drive access that could be a security concern for some people.

Advantages of Screencastify:

  • Fully featured recording app
  • Online drawing capability

Disadvantages of the extension:

  • Failures when trying to log into your account

How to record video on Windows 10: A step-by-step guide

As we promised at the beginning, we’ll show you how to record video on PC with the Windows 10 operating system. Here’s a quick guide to how to use the online recorder but the approach is almost the same for all programs – desktop, online, or browser extension. Only the launch process differs from app to app.

Step 1. Open the Screen Capture web page

Open the tab with the online recorder.

Step 2. Choose the capture parameters

Decide what you want to grab. To record just screen activity without any sound, disable Microphone, Webcam, and Sound. If you're going to record your voice, enable the microphone.

If you want to grab live audio, activate the Sound button.

Click the Start Recording button. Allow access to your webcam and microphone.

Step 3. Record your computer screen

Choose the tab to start recording. The recorder will capture everything that happens on your PC. You can freely switch between tabs.

Notice that the recorder works in Full Screen mode, so it grabs the whole screen. To record just one particular part of your laptop screen, you’ll need to install the desktop version.

Step 4. Save the recording

Stop recording and save the file to your computer. The quality of the video will be much better than if you use the built-in Windows recorder.

The online recorder works with multiple Windows versions.

Summary

We hope you found these reviews helpful. This section summarizes the most important things to consider when choosing a screen recorder for Windows 10.

  • Price: Some screen recorders are free, and others offer both free and paid versions. If you want a free app, make sure it has the features you need. Does it restrict things like recording times and the number of allowed recordings? Consider another app or the PRO version if it falls short, especially if it offers a free trial period.
  • User Interface: No one ever complains that a screen recorder is too easy to use. Make sure the interface is intuitive, uncluttered, and has a gentle learning curve. At the same time, check that the UI has everything you need.
  • Screen recording options: Some tools only record specific areas while others capture the entire screen or both. Also, check that the app offers audio recording as well if that's important to you.
  • Editing tools: Check that the program has editing tools if that matters to you. Some apps offer basic editing like join, trim, and crop. Others have built-in editors or no tools at all.
  • Technical support: Who is going to be available to help you if you have a technical issue? Does the software come with a dedicated customer support team that you can reach out to by online chat or email?
